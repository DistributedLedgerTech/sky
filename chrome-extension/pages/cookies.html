Cookie Policy

Learn how SkyKnet uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you, remember your preferences, and analyze how our blockchain products and services are used.

Last updated: December 28, 2025

SkyKnet, a DBA of Distributed Ledger Technologies Inc. ("SkyKnet," "we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our websites, use our products, or interact with our services. This Cookie Policy explains what these technologies are, why we use them, and your rights to control our use of them. This policy applies to all SkyKnet products and services, including Knexcoin, KnexMail, Ayedex, KnexPay, KnexWallet, and Superknet.

This Cookie Policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy and our Terms of Service, which provide additional information about how we collect, use, and protect your personal data.

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small data files that are placed on your computer, mobile device, or any other device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their websites work, to work more efficiently, and to provide reporting and analytics information. A cookie typically contains the name of the domain from which it originated, the "lifetime" of the cookie (that is, how long it will remain on your device), and a randomly generated unique identifier.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, SkyKnet) are called "first-party cookies." Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called "third-party cookies." Third-party cookies enable third-party features or functionality to be provided on or through the website, such as analytics, interactive content, and advertising. The parties that set these third-party cookies can recognize your computer both when it visits the website in question and also when it visits certain other websites.

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies such as web beacons, pixel tags, local storage objects, and session storage. These technologies are discussed in greater detail in subsequent sections of this policy.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several important reasons. Some cookies are required for technical reasons in order for our websites and products to operate, and we refer to these as "essential" or "strictly necessary" cookies. Other cookies enable us to track and target the interests of our users to enhance the experience on our websites. Third parties may also serve cookies through our websites for analytics, advertising, and other purposes. The specific types of first-party and third-party cookies served through our websites and the purposes they perform are described in detail below.

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following general purposes:

  • Authentication and Security: To verify your account credentials, maintain session integrity, and protect against unauthorized access to your data and accounts.
  • Preferences and Settings: To remember your language preferences, display settings, and other customization choices you have made across our products.
  • Performance and Analytics: To understand how visitors interact with our websites, which pages are most popular, and how our products are used so that we can measure and improve performance.
  • Functionality: To provide enhanced functionality and personalization, including remembering previously entered form data, preferred network configurations, and wallet connection states.
  • Blockchain Operations: To cache blockchain network preferences, transaction history metadata, and node connection states required for the proper functioning of our decentralized applications.

3.4 Targeting / Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement as well as help measure the effectiveness of an advertising campaign. They are usually placed by advertising networks with the website operator's permission. They remember that you have visited a website, and this information is shared with other organizations such as advertisers. Quite often, targeting or advertising cookies will be linked to site functionality provided by the other organization.

At this time, SkyKnet does not use advertising cookies on its primary websites. However, certain third-party services integrated into our products may set their own cookies for advertising or targeting purposes. If we begin using advertising cookies in the future, we will update this Cookie Policy and seek your consent where required by applicable law.

4. First-Party vs. Third-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are cookies that are set by SkyKnet directly. These cookies are used to maintain your session, remember your preferences, and gather analytics about your interaction with our websites. First-party cookies are typically more trusted because they originate from the site you are visiting and are subject to the privacy practices outlined in our Privacy Policy.

Third-party cookies are set by organizations other than SkyKnet. We use a limited number of third-party services that may place cookies on your device. These include Google Analytics for website usage analysis and, potentially, other service providers that enable specific product functionality. We carefully vet all third-party services and only work with providers whose data handling practices meet our standards for user privacy and data protection.

When third-party cookies are used, those third parties are responsible for their own cookies and you should refer to their privacy and cookie policies for information on how they use data collected through their cookies. We do not have access to or control over cookies placed by third parties.

5. Cookie Duration

Cookies can remain on your computer or mobile device for different periods of time. We use two categories based on their lifespan:

5.1 Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that exist only for the duration of your browsing session. They are automatically deleted when you close your web browser. We use session cookies to maintain your login state, carry information across pages within our products, and ensure security during your visit. Session cookies are essential for the proper functioning of features like multi-step transactions in KnexPay or order placement on Ayedex.

5.2 Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period of time specified in the cookie or until you manually delete them. We use persistent cookies to remember your preferences, recognize you when you return to our websites, and collect analytics data over time. The duration of persistent cookies varies depending on their purpose; for example, preference cookies may last up to one year, while analytics cookies set by Google may last up to two years. The exact duration for each cookie is listed in the tables above.

In addition to Google Analytics, our websites and products may integrate with or link to the following third-party services, each of which may set their own cookies:

Each third-party service has its own privacy and cookie policies, which govern how they use the data they collect. We encourage you to review these policies to understand how your information is handled by third parties.

8. Web Beacons & Pixel Tags

In addition to cookies, we may use web beacons (also known as "pixel tags," "clear GIFs," or "tracking pixels") on our websites and in our emails. A web beacon is a tiny, invisible graphic image — typically a one-pixel-by-one-pixel transparent image — that is embedded in a web page or email message. Web beacons are used to monitor user behavior without the user being aware of it.

We use web beacons for the following purposes:

  • Email Analytics: To determine whether emails we send are opened, which links within emails are clicked, and when they are accessed. This helps us measure the effectiveness of our communications and tailor our messages to be more relevant.
  • Website Analytics: To count the number of users who have visited certain pages and to generate statistics about how our websites are used. Web beacons may work in conjunction with cookies to provide a more complete picture of user behavior.
  • Advertising Measurement: If we engage in advertising campaigns, web beacons may be used to determine how many times an advertisement is displayed and how effective it is at generating user interest.

Web beacons do not store information on your device in the way that cookies do. However, by working with cookies, they allow us to recognize you when you return to our websites and can track your activity across sessions. You can typically prevent web beacons from tracking your activity by configuring your email client to block images or by using browser extensions that block tracking technologies.

9. Local Storage & Session Storage

In addition to cookies, modern web browsers provide other mechanisms for storing data locally on your device. Our websites and products may use the following browser storage technologies:

9.1 Local Storage

HTML5 Local Storage allows us to store data in your browser that persists even after you close the browser window. Unlike cookies, local storage data is not sent to the server with every HTTP request, making it more efficient for storing larger amounts of data. We use local storage to store application state information, user preferences, cached content that improves loading performance, and interface customization settings.

9.2 Session Storage

HTML5 Session Storage is similar to local storage but is limited to a single browser tab or window and is automatically cleared when that tab or window is closed. We use session storage for temporary data that is needed only during your current interaction, such as form data you have entered but not yet submitted, multi-step workflow progress, temporary transaction data during the checkout or transfer process, and page navigation history within single-page applications.

You can view, modify, or delete local storage and session storage data through your browser's developer tools. Clearing your browser data will also remove locally stored information. Please note that deleting this data may affect the functionality of our products and may require you to re-enter preferences or re-authenticate.

10. Blockchain-Specific Storage

Given the nature of our blockchain and cryptocurrency products, we use certain specialized storage mechanisms that are unique to decentralized applications:

10.1 Wallet Connection Data

When you connect a cryptocurrency wallet (such as through KnexWallet or when using Ayedex), we may store wallet connection metadata in your browser's local storage. This includes the wallet provider type, the public wallet address you have connected (which is already publicly visible on the blockchain), and your connection preferences. We never store private keys, seed phrases, or any sensitive wallet credentials in cookies, local storage, or any other client-side storage mechanism.

10.2 Transaction Cache

To improve performance and reduce redundant blockchain queries, our products may temporarily cache transaction data and token balances in your browser's local storage or session storage. This cached data is derived entirely from publicly available blockchain information and is used solely to improve the speed and responsiveness of our interfaces. Cached transaction data is periodically refreshed and can be cleared at any time without affecting your actual blockchain assets or transaction history.

10.3 Network Preferences

Our products support multiple blockchain networks and configurations. We store your network preferences — such as your selected chain, custom RPC endpoints, gas price preferences, and slippage tolerance settings — in local storage so that these settings persist across sessions. This ensures a consistent experience every time you return to our products without having to reconfigure network settings.

11. How to Control Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences in several ways. Please note that if you choose to block or delete cookies, some features of our websites and products may not function properly.

11.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. Below are instructions for managing cookies in the most popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Click the three-dot menu in the upper right corner, go to Settings, then Privacy and Security, then Cookies and other site data (or Third-party cookies in newer versions). From here you can block third-party cookies, block all cookies, or clear cookies when you close the browser. You can also manage exceptions for specific sites. Alternatively, type chrome://settings/cookies into the address bar.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Click the three-line menu, go to Settings, then Privacy & Security. Under Enhanced Tracking Protection, you can choose Standard, Strict, or Custom protection levels. Custom mode allows you to specifically control cookies, tracking content, cryptominers, and fingerprinters. You can also manage individual cookies by clicking Manage Data under Cookies and Site Data.
  • Apple Safari: Go to Safari in the menu bar, then Preferences (or Settings on newer versions), then the Privacy tab. You can block all cookies, prevent cross-site tracking, or manage website data to remove cookies from specific sites. Safari also includes Intelligent Tracking Prevention (ITP), which automatically limits third-party cookie tracking.
  • Microsoft Edge: Click the three-dot menu, go to Settings, then Cookies and site permissions, then Manage and delete cookies and site data. You can block third-party cookies, block all cookies, or clear cookies when the browser is closed. You can also add specific sites to allow or block lists for fine-grained control.

11.2 Mobile Devices

On mobile devices, you can manage cookies through your mobile browser settings. On iOS, go to Settings, then Safari, and use the options under Privacy & Security. On Android, open your browser app, access its settings menu, and look for Privacy or Cookies options. Note that the exact steps may vary depending on your device manufacturer and browser version.

11.3 Third-Party Opt-Out Tools

In addition to browser-level controls, you can use third-party tools to manage cookies and tracking across multiple websites:

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out: Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on to prevent Google Analytics data collection.
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI): Visit the NAI's opt-out page to manage cookies from member advertising networks.
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA): Use the DAA's WebChoices tool to opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies.
  • European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EDAA): If you are in the EU, visit the Your Online Choices website to manage your advertising cookie preferences.

12. Do Not Track Signals

Some web browsers offer a "Do Not Track" ("DNT") signal that sends a request to websites indicating that you do not wish to be tracked. At this time, there is no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals, and the technology and policy landscape around DNT continues to evolve.

Currently, our websites do not respond to DNT signals in a standardized way. However, you can use the other cookie control mechanisms described in this policy (browser settings, opt-out tools, and consent preferences) to manage your tracking preferences. We will continue to monitor developments in DNT technology and update our practices accordingly if a uniform standard is adopted.

14. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you choose to disable or delete cookies, some parts of our websites and products may not function properly. Specifically:

  • Authentication: You may be required to log in each time you visit our products, as session cookies will not persist your authentication state.
  • Preferences: Your language, theme, currency, and network preferences may not be remembered, requiring you to reconfigure settings on each visit.
  • Wallet Connections: If you clear local storage, you may need to reconnect your cryptocurrency wallet each time you use Ayedex, KnexWallet, or KnexPay.
  • Transaction History: Cached transaction data and recent address lists may be lost, requiring fresh queries to the blockchain on each visit, which may result in slower loading times.
  • Performance: Without performance cookies, we may be unable to identify and resolve issues that affect your experience with our products.
  • Security Features: Certain security features, such as device recognition for two-factor authentication, may be impacted, potentially resulting in additional verification steps on each login.

We strongly recommend keeping essential cookies enabled to ensure the best possible experience when using SkyKnet products and services. You can selectively disable non-essential cookies (such as analytics and functional cookies) while keeping essential cookies active.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have specific rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the ePrivacy Directive (often referred to as the "Cookie Law") regarding cookies and similar technologies.

Under these regulations:

  • We will obtain your explicit consent before placing any non-essential cookies on your device.
  • Essential cookies may be placed without consent, as they are necessary for the provision of the service you have requested.
  • You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time, and we will provide clear mechanisms for doing so.
  • We will provide clear and comprehensive information about the cookies we use before asking for your consent.
  • We will not use cookie walls that force you to accept cookies in order to access our websites.
  • Your consent preferences will be stored and honored for a reasonable period, after which we may ask you to renew your consent.

The legal basis for our use of essential cookies is our legitimate interest in providing a secure, functional website. For non-essential cookies, the legal basis is your informed consent. For more information about your data protection rights under the GDPR, please refer to our Privacy Policy.

  • Right to Know: You have the right to know what personal information is collected through cookies and other tracking technologies, and how it is used and shared.
  • Right to Delete: You may request deletion of personal information collected through cookies, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Opt-Out: If we sell or share personal information collected through cookies for cross-context behavioral advertising, you have the right to opt out. At this time, SkyKnet does not sell personal information or use cookies for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights, including by charging different prices, providing different quality of service, or denying you service.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@skyknet.com.
